## SDE

My team is a networking team, which is responsible for the last networking layer of devices that can be seen on a `trace` call to an EC2 instance. This layer is responsible for moving traffic into
a different group of workers which encapsulates the traffic to give the right routing information to reach your virtualised EC2 instance, given your elastic IP.

My team developed a SDN controller for this layer, and I got started by building safety mechanisms into this controller. This involved designing patterns for making these prechecks and postchecks,
creating rollback mechanisms and create extensible mechanisms which handle cross-cutting concerns. These are things like logging, error handling. This mechanism was extended by others for new concerns like
configuration overrides to skip or alter these checks at runtime. I also ensured onboarding these prechecks and postchecks is painless by providing factories for creating
them with full monitoring and orchestration. 

I also defined, advocated for, architected and implemented a new service responsible for monitoring network devices by listening to a data source for updates to the capacity of our devices
to carry traffic when compared to its maximum traffic. This involved listening to stakeholders including senior engineers and network engineers
to define and implement an alert which addressed common previous issues which were hard to remediate on a device-to-device basis.
This instead monitored traffic pathways, and alerted us if they degraded past a reasonable threshold. This service caught multiple situations which required our attention to fix, and helped improve other services
which would unwittingly cause the underlying issues engineers discovered through the alerts.

## Internship

I designed, defended and implemented a system architecture over 6 months to analyse a given team's on-call alerts. This service was
infinitely extensible for its clients - as they were able to create simple serverless functions to enrich the data ingested by my service. This data would then feed a dashboard, which comes with automatic insights and data-driven suggestions. This saved at least 2 hours weekly
of manual data processing by senior engineers in every team that leverages it. I owned the CI/CD, testing, monitoring, and validation
of this cloud-based application.

I also got a taste of working on Aurora Platform. I developed a small piece of infrastructure required for IPv6. This work aided
multiple services running under the Aurora brand, such as Aurora Postgres and Aurora Serverless.

I got to experience what it is like to be on a highly mature team full of talent - especially ones that don't often agree. I learned about how to
ensure large-scale programs continue to be functional, healthy, and easy to maintain. I learned these best practices and am delighted to carry
them forward throughout my career.
